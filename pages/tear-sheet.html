Validated Momentum

Cross-Sectional Momentum

Ranks assets by trailing risk-adjusted returns while skipping the most recent month to reduce short-term reversal noise.

Factor thesis

Assets that have outperformed over the intermediate horizon often continue to outperform, especially when the most recent month is excluded to reduce short-term reversal noise.

Construction note

Cross-sectional ranking is applied after volatility adjustment and sector neutralisation. The design is simple on purpose: the factor should be easy to explain and difficult to overfit.

Interpretation

Momentum remains attractive because it is persistent enough to measure in data, yet not so simple that the signal becomes a pure market beta proxy.

Time-Series Momentum

Factor thesis

Markets that have been trending upward or downward often continue moving in the same direction over intermediate horizons.

Construction note

The sign of trailing 12-month returns is applied independently to each asset. This differs from cross-sectional ranking and can diversify the overall factor stack.

Interpretation

Time-series momentum is especially useful as a complement to cross-sectional momentum because the sources of return are related but not identical.
