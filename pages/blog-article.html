Why We Report IC Rather Than Backtest Returns

A signal should be judged by how much predictive content it contains, not only by how impressive a portfolio backtest looks after repeated experimentation.

Alpha Factor Research Team November 2024 8 min read

The seductive appeal of Sharpe ratios

When many factor specifications are tested and only the best backtest is published, the reported Sharpe often overstates the true level of predictive content.

A signal can look excellent after portfolio construction even when the underlying relationship is weak or unstable. That is why the note argues for a signal-level metric first.

What the information coefficient measures

IC is the rank correlation between the signal and the subsequent realised return across assets at each rebalance period. It is easier to interpret, less sensitive to portfolio construction, and more directly tied to the question the signal is supposed to answer.

Practical takeaway

A positive, statistically significant IC is evidence that the signal contains predictive content independent of any one portfolio recipe.

Our reporting standard

For each factor, we report mean IC, ICIR, an adjusted t-statistic, a simple long-short Sharpe, turnover, and decay curves across holding horizons.
