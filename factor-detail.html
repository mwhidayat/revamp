Economic Rationale

The momentum premium is one of the most replicated anomalies in empirical asset pricing. Assets that have outperformed in the recent past tend to continue outperforming over a medium horizon (1–12 months). The effect is attributed to behavioural mechanisms — underreaction to news, investor herding — as well as structural frictions that slow arbitrage.

The exclusion of the most recent month reduces contamination from microstructure effects and short-term reversal, which are well-documented at the one-month horizon. Sector neutralisation isolates stock-level momentum from industry-level return effects, which have a distinct and separately estimable signal.

Validation Notes

Known Failure Mode: Momentum strategies exhibit "crash risk" following sharp market recoveries. The factor can suffer significant losses in periods of rapid regime reversal (e.g., March–May 2009, April 2020). Users should consider conditioning on volatility regime when sizing momentum exposure.

Out-of-sample testing conducted on data from Jan 2024 – Dec 2024. OOS Sharpe of 0.72 is after estimated transaction costs at 5 bps per side. IC of 0.041 is statistically significant (t = 3.2) after Bonferroni correction for 8 candidate specifications tested.
