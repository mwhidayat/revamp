About Alpha Factor

An independent quantitative research team publishing validated factor datasets for systematic investors.

Our Mission

Research-Grade Data, Without Compromise

Alpha Factor was founded on a straightforward observation: the gap between institutional quantitative research and commercially available data products is wider than it needs to be. Most retail-facing factor datasets are underdocumented, overfitted to in-sample performance, or derived from methodology that has never been subjected to rigorous independent review.

We believe that systematic investors at every scale deserve access to factors that have been held to academic standards of statistical validation. That means clear documentation of construction rules, honest reporting of out-of-sample performance, and an explicit account of where a factor may fail.

We do not overstate the predictive power of our datasets. We publish decay curves, regime-conditional performance, and robustness check results alongside every factor. If a factor shows anomalous fragility, we say so.

Core Values

Intellectual Honesty

We report failures and limitations as clearly as we report successes.

Reproducibility

Every construction rule is documented with sufficient specificity to reproduce the signal independently.

Transparency

We publish methodology notes, known failure modes, and regime-conditional performance for every factor.

Independence

Our research team operates independently of commercial incentives. We reject factors that fail validation regardless of their sales potential.

Methodology

What "Validated" Means

The word "validated" carries a specific technical meaning at Alpha Factor. We use it only when a factor satisfies all five of the following criteria.

01 — OUT-OF-SAMPLE TESTING

Sequential, Walk-Forward Evaluation

All reported performance statistics are computed exclusively on data that was never accessible during factor development. We use sequential walk-forward splits, not random cross-validation, to respect the time structure of financial data. A factor is not considered validated until it demonstrates positive performance on at least two non-overlapping out-of-sample windows.

02 — STATISTICAL SIGNIFICANCE

Corrected for Multiple Comparisons

We apply Bonferroni-corrected significance thresholds given the number of candidate specifications evaluated during development. A factor that is marginally significant after testing thirty variants is not treated as significant. We report the adjusted t-statistic alongside raw values and require a minimum information ratio threshold in out-of-sample periods.

03 — DECAY ANALYSIS

Information Content Across Holding Horizons

We measure the IC (information coefficient) of every factor across holding horizons from daily to annual. Factors with unexplained discontinuities in decay — which often indicate data artefacts or look-ahead bias — are rejected. Decay curves are published in each tearsheet so subscribers can calibrate holding periods appropriately.

04 — ROBUSTNESS CHECKS

Sub-Period and Specification Stress Tests

Each factor is re-estimated across meaningful sub-periods (pre- and post-2008, crisis vs. expansion, high vs. low volatility regimes). We also perturb construction parameters to test whether the signal is materially sensitive to specific implementation choices. A factor that survives only with exact parameter values is not considered robust.

05 — OVERFIT REJECTION

Structural Simplicity Requirement

Factors with excessive parametrisation relative to their economic rationale are rejected even when they pass statistical tests. We apply a structural complexity penalty analogous to AIC/BIC in model selection. We prefer simple, economically motivated specifications over complex ones with marginal in-sample performance improvements.

The Team

Quant Research Team

The Alpha Factor research team comprises quantitative analysts with backgrounds spanning systematic strategy development, academic empirical finance, and portfolio construction.

We do not publish individual researcher names or profiles. Our datasets stand on their methodology, not personal reputation. Research notes are attributed to the Alpha Factor Research team collectively.

Quant Research
